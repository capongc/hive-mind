# AI Native

**Source:** raw/assets/ai-native.md
**Date ingested:** 2026-04-18
**Type:** notes

## Summary

Defines what it means to be an AI-native software development team — one that cannot function without AI — and synthesizes perspectives from leading practitioners and organizations. Establishes the core principle that every team member's job is to automate their own job.

## Key Claims

- The hard line between AI-enhanced and AI-native: one has driver assist, the other is self-driving
- Remove the AI from an AI-native team and the team stops working entirely
- The competitive unit is no longer the team alone — it is **team × its agents**
- 2–5x output is becoming the baseline expectation for AI-native teams
- 65% of developers expect their roles to be redefined by 2026 — moving toward architecture, integration, and AI-enabled decision-making
- Humans shift to architecture, intent-setting, judgment, and orchestration — not execution
