The V-Bounce: AI-Native Software Development Methodology

The **V-Bounce** model was proposed by Cory Hymel of Crowdbotics in the white paper, _"The AI-Native Software Development Lifecycle: A Theoretical and Practical New Methodology"_. It is a reimagining of the traditional V-model used in systems engineering, adapted for a workflow where AI serves as the primary implementation engine.

Core Concepts

- **The "Bounce":** The name refers to the near-instantaneous time spent at the bottom of the "V" (the implementation/coding phase). Because AI generates code so rapidly, teams "bounce" quickly from design to validation.
- **Human Role Shift:** Humans move from being primary "creators" to "verifiers" and "validators" who operate at the top of the V, focusing on high-level requirements and architecture.
- **Real-Time Test Generation:** Unlike the traditional model, V-Bounce leverages AI to generate corresponding test cases in real-time as natural language requirements are input. This ensures that every requirement is testable the moment it is conceived.
- **Traceability by Design:** The simultaneous creation of requirements and tests creates an inherent traceability matrix, making it easier to manage complexity and update the system when requirements change.
